# Learning & Development

Cybersecurity skills are perishable. Continuous learning isn't optional—it's how you stay effective against evolving threats.

***

### The Reality

The threat landscape shifts constantly. Tools change, adversary TTPs evolve, and yesterday's expertise becomes tomorrow's gap. Structured learning keeps skills current and careers progressing.

### Building a Learning Plan

#### 1. Assess Current State

* What's your current role and target role?
* Where are your skill gaps relative to job requirements?
* What tools does your environment use?

#### 2. Set Focused Goals

* One certification per 6–12 months
* One new tool or technique per quarter
* Regular hands-on practice (2–4 hours/week minimum)

#### 3. Mix Learning Methods

<table><thead><tr><th width="138">Method</th><th>Purpose</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>Courses</td><td>Structured knowledge acquisition</td></tr><tr><td>Labs</td><td>Hands-on skill building</td></tr><tr><td>Projects</td><td>Applied learning, portfolio building</td></tr><tr><td>Reading</td><td>Threat intel, research, staying current</td></tr><tr><td>Community</td><td>Networking, mentorship, exposure to new ideas</td></tr></tbody></table>

#### 4. Track Progress

* Document completed training
* Maintain a home lab for experimentation
* Build a portfolio (blog, GitHub, write-ups)

### Staying Current

* **Threat intel feeds** — Follow threat reports from Mandiant, CrowdStrike, Microsoft, Unit 42
* **Security news** — BleepingComputer, The Record, Risky Business podcast
* **Research** — Read incident reports, malware analyses, detection blogs
* **Community** — Twitter/X infosec community, Discord servers, Reddit r/blueteamsec

***

### Quick Wins

1. **Start a TryHackMe path** — SOC Level 1 or Cyber Defence
2. **Set up a home lab** — Basic Windows/Linux VMs for tool practice
3. **Pick one certification** — Aligned to your next career step
4. **Join a community** — Discord, local meetup, or online forum
5. **Schedule learning time** — Blocked calendar time, treated as non-negotiable
6. **Document as you learn** — Notes, blog posts, or internal knowledge base
